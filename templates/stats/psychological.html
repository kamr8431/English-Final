“In general, those who seek and achieve integration have better psychological adaptation, life satisfaction, self-esteem, and they have fewer mental health problems, lower levels of stress, anxiety and depression. They also found differences in personality, with integration being associated with lower levels of neuroticism, psychoticism, aggression and anger, and higher levels of extraversion, openness and agreeableness, This pattern of psychological characteristics clearly demonstrates the advantages of using the integration strategy during the acculturation process”(Berry)

  • Berry challenges assumption that assimilation is the only path to success in a new country
  • Berry suggests that preserving aspects of one’s original identity can contribute positively to long-term well being and adaption
  • By listing all the psychological benefits associated with integration compared to assimilation, Berry expands the definition of success beyond economic achievement and social status
  • This study demonstrates that when we take our definition of success outside the very limited view of the American Dream, integration can actually be a more advantageous strategy than assimilation
